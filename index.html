New Zealand is part of the continent Oceania. New Zealand consists of two main islands, the North & South Island,but also consists of over 700 smaller islands. It has a population of about 5 Million people. There are 5 times as many sheep as there are people. That means that there are about 25 million sheep. New Zealand's national bird is the Kiwi bird. The Kiwi bird is a type of flightless bird. There are 5 different Kiwi bird species. New Zealand is around 500 million years old. The drive to the coast is never longer than an hour. 30% of New Zealand is a national reserve. It has 3 main languages, English, Maori, and New Zealand Sign language.

Wellington is the Capital of New Zealand. It is located on the North Island and has a population of 212,700 people. Wellington is the Southernmost Capital in the World. Has over 400 cafes within the city. Wellington was voted the World's 13th best city for quality of living in a 2012 Mercer 'International Quality Of Living Survey'. It's the world's windiest city by average wind speed. Wellington is the most remote capital city in the world. It has some cool events and festivals. Wellington also has the largest Kite festival in all of New Zealand. It has an average winter temperature of 50-60°F (10-15°C) and 70-90°F (20-30°C). And averages 122 days of rain in New Zealand.

Christchurch is the biggest city on the South island of New Zealand. And is the second biggest city overall. It has a population of 381,500 people. It has many wonderful photography experiences. It is also the oldest city within New Zealand. It is known as the Garden City. This is because of all its wonderful gardens throughout the city. Christchurch is also home to New Zealand's longestand highest ziplines. Christchurch has an earthquake in 2011 with a magnitude of 6.3. The Maori name for Christchurch is Ōtautahi. Archaeological evidence suggests Christchurch was first settled by moa-hunting tribes as early as AD 1000.

Auckland is the biggest city in all of New Zealand. With a population of 1.657 million people. It is on New Zealand's North Island. The Maori name for Auckland is Tamaki Makaurau. In Auckland there are around 50 volcanoes spread all throughout the city. These volcanoes are dormant though and have a 0.1% chance of eruption. Auckland also has the largest polynesian population in the world. Auckland also has one of the Southern Hemisphere's tallest man-made towers. This tower reaches 328 meters tall (1,075 feet). Auckland was once the capital of New Zealand in 1842, until Wellington became the new capital in 1865.
